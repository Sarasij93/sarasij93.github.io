Research Interests

My research interests lie in Commutative Algebra. I work with Noetherian commutative local rings and the structure theory of modules over such rings. In particular, I am interested in studying the notions of torsion, reflexivity, Ulrichness of modules, amongst other related topics. These have involved a variety of tools such as local cohomology, Hilbert-Samuel Multiplicity, numerical semi-group rings and Frobenius numbers (this is related to the Chicken McNugget Problem!), derived categories and Homological Algebra.

I am equally interested in the exploring the applications of Linear Algebra in Data Science and Machine Learning. During my PhD, I also was engaged in reading and reasearching various topics in Cryptography with Prof. David Wu.
